Based upon the newborn's gestational age at birth, development will vary. The behaviors a newborn will exhibit at various weeks include the following. Click each week to view the various stages of a newborn's development.
The infant will exhibit jerky movements and physiological instability during handling and in response to noise or a wet diaper.
The infant will exhibit low muscle tone. Non-nutritive sucking begins at 28 weeks.
Behavioral stress signs will occur when handling decreases. The rooting reflex develops.
Signs of a sleep-wake cycle begin. Eye opening will increase in dim light. Infants at 32 weeks should be evaluated for readiness of nipple feeding, if medical condition permits.
The infant will exhibit increased smooth, controlled movements. Short periods of music during wake time may be tolerated. Infants may awaken for feedings.
The infant will have a flexed trunk and extremities. Vision is best at a distance of eight to 10 inches. Overall interaction states are well-defined.
